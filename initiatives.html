Sustainability Initiatives in the Printing Industry.

The printing industry is making strides towards sustainability by implementing various initiatives. This page will explore some of the most popular initiatives in the printing industry, including ethical sourcing, waste reduction, and energy efficiency. These initiatives not only benefit the environment but also help businesses save costs and improve their overall sustainability performance.

Most Popular Initiatives

Image obtained from wistia.com

Ethical Sourcing

  • Ethical sourcing is the process of prioritizing materials from responsibly managed forests and suppliers with ethical labour practices.
  • Using paper made from recycled materials to reduce reliance on non-renewable resources and minimize waste.
  • Explore alternatives such as hemp, bamboo, sugarcane, and reclaimed straw for paper production.
  • Using responsibly sourced inks that are not harmful to the environment to be disposed of (GelatoConnect, 2024).

Image obtained from storyblok.com

Waste Reduction and Recycling

  • Tracking print usage can help spot patterns in operations where excessive or unnecessary printing is occurring.
  • Eco-friendly inks, such as vegetable or soy-based inks, are more biodegradable compared to traditional inks.
  • Opting for recycled paper contributes to waste reduction and the conservation of our forests.
  • Previously iterated, double-sided printing can drastically cut paper waste by 50% (GelatoConnect, 2024).

Image obtained from storyblok.com

Energy Efficiency and Digital Technologies

  • Reducing the reliance on hard copy proofs can provide a significant decrease in paper usage. Digital proofs are cost-effective and environmentally friendly.
  • By adopting efficient print-management software, organizations can effectively reduce their carbon footprint without sacrificing operations.
  • Companies can optimize their inventories by only stocking materials that are necessary; this solution is often achieved with state-of-the-art software.
  • The implementation of real-time analytics can also help organizations save energy. Not only is this better for the environment, it reduces the cost of energy delivery bills (GelatoConnect, 2024).

Who should do the most work?

While it is only fair that the largest printing companies take the most responsibility, it is also important to note that smaller companies can make a difference too. In addition to smaller companies, individuals can also make a difference by reducing their own printing. The average person prints 7,000 pages per year, which is equivalent to 1.5 trees (Nestor, 2024). If everyone reduced their printing by 50%, we could save 1.5 million trees per year! (Nestor, 2024). Wouldn't that be a great start?
